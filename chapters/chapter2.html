Chapter 2: Acids, Bases and Salts

Understanding acids, bases, pH scale, and neutralization reactions

1. Understanding Acids and Bases

Acids

Substances that taste sour, turn blue litmus red, and release H⁺ ions in aqueous solution.

Bases

Substances that taste bitter, turn red litmus blue, and release OH⁻ ions in aqueous solution.

Common Examples:

Acids: HCl, H₂SO₄, HNO₃, CH₃COOH
Bases: NaOH, KOH, Ca(OH)₂, NH₄OH
Example:

Hydrochloric acid (HCl) releases H⁺ ions in water: HCl → H⁺ + Cl⁻

2. pH Scale and Indicators

pH Scale

A scale from 0 to 14 that measures the acidity or basicity of a solution. pH = -log[H⁺]

pH Values

  • pH < 7: Acidic solutions
  • pH = 7: Neutral solutions
  • pH > 7: Basic solutions

Common Indicators:

  • Litmus: Red in acids, blue in bases
  • Phenolphthalein: Colorless in acids, pink in bases
  • Methyl Orange: Red in acids, yellow in bases
  • Universal Indicator: Shows different colors for different pH values

3. Properties of Acids and Bases

Properties of Acids:

  • Sour taste
  • Turn blue litmus red
  • Conduct electricity in aqueous solution
  • React with metals to produce hydrogen gas
  • React with carbonates to produce CO₂ gas

Properties of Bases:

  • Bitter taste
  • Turn red litmus blue
  • Conduct electricity in aqueous solution
  • Feel soapy to touch
  • React with acids to form salt and water
Example:
Acid + Metal: 2HCl + Zn → ZnCl₂ + H₂
Acid + Carbonate: 2HCl + CaCO₃ → CaCl₂ + H₂O + CO₂

4. Neutralization Reactions

Neutralization

A reaction between an acid and a base to form salt and water.

General Equation

Acid + Base → Salt + Water

Common Neutralization Reactions:

  • HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O
  • H₂SO₄ + 2KOH → K₂SO₄ + 2H₂O
  • HNO₃ + KOH → KNO₃ + H₂O
  • CH₃COOH + NaOH → CH₃COONa + H₂O
Example:

When hydrochloric acid reacts with sodium hydroxide, sodium chloride (common salt) and water are formed.

5. Salts and their Properties

Salts

Ionic compounds formed by the neutralization of acids and bases.

Types of Salts:

  • Normal Salts: Formed by complete neutralization
  • Acid Salts: Contain replaceable hydrogen atoms
  • Basic Salts: Contain hydroxide ions
  • Double Salts: Contain two different cations or anions

Properties of Salts:

  • Usually crystalline solids
  • High melting and boiling points
  • Conduct electricity in molten state or aqueous solution
  • May be soluble or insoluble in water
Examples:
Normal Salt: NaCl (Sodium Chloride)
Acid Salt: NaHSO₄ (Sodium Hydrogen Sulphate)
Basic Salt: Cu(OH)Cl (Copper Hydroxide Chloride)

Important Formulas and Definitions

Key Points to Remember

  • pH scale ranges from 0 to 14
  • Acids have pH < 7, bases have pH > 7
  • Neutralization always produces salt and water
  • Strong acids and bases conduct electricity well
  • Indicators change color based on pH

Important Chemical Equations:

  • pH = -log[H⁺]
  • pOH = -log[OH⁻]
  • pH + pOH = 14
  • Kw = [H⁺][OH⁻] = 10⁻¹⁴
