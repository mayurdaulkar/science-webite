1. What are Life Processes?

Life processes are the basic functions performed by living organisms to maintain life.

Definition: Life Processes

The basic functions that all living organisms perform to maintain life, including nutrition, respiration, transportation, and excretion.

Basic Life Processes:

  • Nutrition: Taking in food and utilizing it
  • Respiration: Releasing energy from food
  • Transportation: Moving materials within the body
  • Excretion: Removing waste products

2. Nutrition

Nutrition is the process of taking in food and utilizing it for growth and development.

Definition: Nutrition

The process of obtaining and utilizing food for growth, repair, and energy.

Types of Nutrition:

  • Autotrophic Nutrition: Organisms that make their own food
  • Heterotrophic Nutrition: Organisms that depend on others for food

Diagram: Photosynthesis Process

Photosynthesis Equation:

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light Energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

3. Respiration

Respiration is the process of releasing energy from food.

Definition: Respiration

The process of breaking down food to release energy for life activities.

Types of Respiration:

  • Aerobic Respiration: Respiration in presence of oxygen
  • Anaerobic Respiration: Respiration in absence of oxygen

Diagram: Human Respiratory System

Respiration Equation:

C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Energy

4. Transportation

Transportation is the process of moving materials within the body.

Definition: Transportation

The process of moving nutrients, oxygen, and waste products within the body.

Transportation in Humans:

  • Blood: Carries oxygen, nutrients, and waste
  • Heart: Pumps blood throughout the body
  • Blood Vessels: Arteries, veins, and capillaries

Diagram: Human Circulatory System

5. Excretion

Excretion is the process of removing waste products from the body.

Definition: Excretion

The process of removing metabolic waste products from the body.

Excretory Organs:

  • Kidneys: Filter blood and remove waste
  • Lungs: Remove carbon dioxide
  • Skin: Remove water and salts through sweat
  • Liver: Remove toxic substances

Diagram: Human Excretory System

Functions of Kidneys:

  • Filter blood and remove waste
  • Maintain water balance
  • Regulate salt concentration
  • Remove excess substances
