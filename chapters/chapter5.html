1. Early Attempts at Classification

Scientists tried to classify elements based on their properties and atomic masses.

Dobereiner's Triads

Groups of three elements with similar properties where the atomic mass of the middle element is approximately the average of the other two.

Example Triads:

  • Li (7), Na (23), K (39)
  • Ca (40), Sr (88), Ba (137)

2. Mendeleev's Periodic Table

Dmitri Mendeleev arranged elements in order of increasing atomic masses and grouped them by similar properties.

Mendeleev's Achievements:

  • Left gaps for undiscovered elements
  • Predicted properties of missing elements
  • Grouped elements with similar properties

3. The Modern Periodic Table

Based on atomic number (number of protons) rather than atomic mass.

Definition: Atomic Number

The number of protons in the nucleus of an atom.

Modern Periodic Law:

"The properties of elements are a periodic function of their atomic numbers."

4. Trends in the Modern Periodic Table

Atomic Size

Definition: Atomic Radius

Half the distance between the nuclei of two identical atoms bonded together.

Trends in Atomic Size:

  • Across a period: Decreases (left to right)
  • Down a group: Increases (top to bottom)

Metallic Character

Metallic Character Trends:

  • Decreases across a period (left to right)
  • Increases down a group (top to bottom)
