1. Animals - Nervous System

The nervous system helps animals respond to changes in their environment.

Definition: Nervous System

A complex network of nerves and cells that transmit signals between different parts of the body.

Functions of Nervous System:

  • Receives information from environment
  • Processes and interprets information
  • Sends commands to muscles and glands
  • Coordinates body activities

Diagram: Human Nervous System

2. What happens in Reflex Actions?

Reflex actions are automatic responses that occur without conscious thought.

Definition: Reflex Action

A rapid, automatic response to a stimulus that occurs without conscious thought.

Examples of Reflex Actions:

  • Pulling hand away from hot object
  • Blinking when something approaches eye
  • Knee jerk reflex
  • Sneezing

Diagram: Reflex Arc

3. Human Brain

The brain is the control center of the nervous system.

Definition: Brain

The organ that controls all body functions and processes information from the senses.

Parts of the Brain:

  • Cerebrum: Controls thinking, memory, and voluntary actions
  • Cerebellum: Controls balance and coordination
  • Medulla: Controls involuntary actions like breathing

Diagram: Human Brain Structure

4. Coordination in Plants

Plants coordinate their activities through chemical signals called hormones.

Definition: Plant Hormones

Chemical substances that regulate plant growth and development.

Types of Plant Hormones:

  • Auxins: Control cell elongation and growth
  • Gibberellins: Promote stem elongation
  • Cytokinins: Promote cell division
  • Abscisic Acid: Inhibits growth

Diagram: Plant Responses

5. Hormones in Animals

Hormones are chemical messengers that regulate various body functions.

Definition: Hormones

Chemical substances secreted by endocrine glands that regulate body functions.

Important Hormones:

  • Insulin: Regulates blood sugar levels
  • Adrenaline: Prepares body for emergency
  • Thyroxine: Controls metabolism
  • Growth Hormone: Controls growth

Diagram: Endocrine System

Hormone Action:

Hormone + Receptor → Cellular Response

Example: Insulin Action

When blood sugar is high, pancreas secretes insulin which helps cells absorb glucose from blood.
