Sources of Energy

Energy is the capacity to do work. In this chapter, we will learn about various sources of energy, their characteristics, advantages, and disadvantages.

Key Topics

  • What is a good source of energy?
  • Conventional sources of energy
  • Non-conventional sources of energy
  • Environmental consequences
  • How long will an energy source last?

What is a Good Source of Energy?

Characteristics of a Good Source of Energy:

  • High calorific value: Should release large amount of energy per unit mass
  • Easy availability: Should be easily accessible
  • Easy storage and transport: Should be convenient to store and transport
  • Economical: Should be cost-effective
  • Safe to use: Should not cause harm to environment or living beings
  • Clean and pollution-free: Should not cause environmental pollution

Conventional Sources of Energy

1. Fossil Fuels

Definition: Fossil fuels are formed from the remains of dead plants and animals over millions of years.

Types:
  • Coal: Formed from dead plants buried under earth's crust
  • Petroleum: Formed from dead marine organisms
  • Natural Gas: Found along with petroleum deposits
Advantages:
  • High calorific value
  • Easy to transport
  • Well-established technology
Disadvantages:
  • Non-renewable (limited supply)
  • Cause air pollution
  • Release greenhouse gases
  • Expensive

2. Thermal Power Plant

Working Principle: Heat energy is converted to electrical energy.

Process:
  1. Fossil fuels are burnt to heat water
  2. Steam is produced at high pressure
  3. Steam rotates the turbine
  4. Turbine rotates the generator
  5. Generator produces electricity
Disadvantages:
  • Low efficiency (30-40%)
  • High fuel consumption
  • Environmental pollution

3. Hydro Power Plant

Working Principle: Kinetic energy of flowing water is converted to electrical energy.

Process:
  1. Water is stored at a height (dam)
  2. Water flows down through pipes
  3. Flowing water rotates the turbine
  4. Turbine rotates the generator
  5. Generator produces electricity
Advantages:
  • Renewable source
  • No fuel cost
  • No pollution
  • High efficiency
Disadvantages:
  • High initial cost
  • Dependent on rainfall
  • Displacement of people
  • Ecological changes

Non-Conventional Sources of Energy

1. Solar Energy

Definition: Energy obtained from the sun's radiation.

Solar Cooker:
  • Uses concave mirror to focus sunlight
  • Temperature can reach 100-140Â°C
  • No fuel cost
  • Environment-friendly
Solar Cell:
  • Converts solar energy to electrical energy
  • Made of silicon
  • Used in calculators, satellites
  • Expensive but long-lasting
Advantages:
  • Renewable and abundant
  • No pollution
  • No fuel cost
  • Low maintenance
Disadvantages:
  • Intermittent (not available at night)
  • Low efficiency
  • High initial cost
  • Requires large area

2. Wind Energy

Definition: Energy obtained from moving air (wind).

Wind Mill:
  • Uses wind to rotate blades
  • Blades rotate the turbine
  • Turbine rotates the generator
  • Generator produces electricity
Advantages:
  • Renewable source
  • No fuel cost
  • No pollution
  • Low maintenance
Disadvantages:
  • Intermittent (depends on wind)
  • Requires large area
  • High initial cost
  • Noise pollution

3. Biomass Energy

Definition: Energy obtained from organic matter (plants, animal waste).

Biogas:
  • Produced by anaerobic decomposition
  • Main component: Methane (CHâ)
  • Used for cooking and lighting
  • Slurry is used as fertilizer
Advantages:
  • Renewable source
  • Reduces waste
  • Provides fertilizer
  • No pollution
Disadvantages:
  • Low calorific value
  • Requires large area
  • Intermittent supply

4. Tidal Energy

Definition: Energy obtained from the rise and fall of sea tides.

Working:
  • Dam is built across a narrow opening
  • Water flows in during high tide
  • Water flows out during low tide
  • Flowing water rotates turbine
Advantages:
  • Renewable source
  • Predictable
  • No pollution
Disadvantages:
  • Limited to coastal areas
  • High initial cost
  • Affects marine life

5. Geothermal Energy

Definition: Energy obtained from the heat inside the earth.

Working:
  • Hot water/steam from earth's interior
  • Steam rotates turbine
  • Turbine rotates generator
  • Generator produces electricity
Advantages:
  • Renewable source
  • No fuel cost
  • No pollution
  • Continuous supply
Disadvantages:
  • Limited to specific areas
  • High initial cost
  • May release harmful gases

6. Nuclear Energy

Definition: Energy obtained from nuclear reactions (fission/fusion).

Nuclear Fission:
  • Heavy nucleus splits into lighter nuclei
  • Releases large amount of energy
  • Used in nuclear power plants
  • Fuel: Uranium-235
Advantages:
  • High energy output
  • Small fuel requirement
  • No air pollution
Disadvantages:
  • Radioactive waste
  • Risk of accidents
  • High initial cost
  • Limited fuel supply

Energy Conservation

Why Energy Conservation is Important:

  • Fossil fuels are limited and depleting
  • Reduces environmental pollution
  • Saves money
  • Reduces dependence on imports
  • Ensures energy security

Ways to Conserve Energy:

  • Use energy-efficient appliances
  • Switch off lights when not needed
  • Use public transport
  • Use renewable energy sources
  • Proper insulation of buildings
  • Regular maintenance of equipment

Important Definitions

Energy: The capacity to do work
Calorific Value: Amount of heat energy released when 1 kg of fuel is completely burnt
Renewable Energy: Energy sources that can be replenished naturally
Non-renewable Energy: Energy sources that cannot be replenished once used
Biomass: Organic matter derived from living or recently living organisms
Nuclear Fission: Process in which a heavy nucleus splits into lighter nuclei

Important Points to Remember

  • Fossil fuels are non-renewable sources of energy
  • Solar energy is the most abundant source of energy
  • Nuclear energy has the highest energy density
  • Biomass is a renewable source of energy
  • Wind energy is clean and pollution-free
  • Hydroelectric power has the highest efficiency
  • Geothermal energy is available 24/7
  • Tidal energy is predictable and renewable
  • Energy conservation is as important as energy production
  • India has great potential for solar and wind energy

Sample Questions

Q1. What are the characteristics of a good source of energy?

Answer: A good source of energy should have high calorific value, be easily available, easy to store and transport, economical, safe to use, and clean and pollution-free.

Q2. Why are fossil fuels called non-renewable sources of energy?

Answer: Fossil fuels are called non-renewable because they take millions of years to form and once used, they cannot be replenished in a short time period.

Q3. What are the advantages of solar energy?

Answer: Solar energy is renewable, abundant, pollution-free, has no fuel cost, requires low maintenance, and is available everywhere.

Q4. How does a windmill work?

Answer: Wind rotates the blades of the windmill, which in turn rotates the turbine. The turbine rotates the generator, which produces electricity.

Q5. What is biogas? How is it produced?

Answer: Biogas is a mixture of gases (mainly methane) produced by the anaerobic decomposition of organic matter like animal waste, plant waste, etc. It is produced in biogas plants where organic matter is decomposed by bacteria in the absence of oxygen.
