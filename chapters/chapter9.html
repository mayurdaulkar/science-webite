1. Accumulation of Variation During Reproduction

Variations accumulate during reproduction and are passed on to offspring.

Definition: Variation

Differences in characteristics between individuals of the same species.

Sources of Variation:

  • Genetic recombination during sexual reproduction
  • Mutations in DNA
  • Environmental factors
  • Random assortment of chromosomes

2. Heredity

Heredity is the transmission of characteristics from parents to offspring.

Definition: Heredity

The transmission of genetic characteristics from parents to offspring.

Basic Terms:

  • Gene: Unit of heredity
  • Allele: Alternative form of a gene
  • Genotype: Genetic makeup
  • Phenotype: Physical appearance

3. Inherited Traits

Traits that are passed from parents to offspring through genes.

Examples of Inherited Traits:

  • Eye color
  • Hair color
  • Height
  • Blood group
  • Skin color

Characteristics of Inherited Traits:

  • Controlled by genes
  • Passed from generation to generation
  • Can be dominant or recessive
  • May show variation

4. Rules for the Inheritance of Traits

Mendel's laws govern the inheritance of traits.

Mendel's Laws:

  • Law of Segregation: Alleles separate during gamete formation
  • Law of Independent Assortment: Genes for different traits assort independently

Monohybrid Cross:

TT (Tall) × tt (Short) → Tt (Tall)

F₂ Generation: 3 Tall : 1 Short

Example: Pea Plant Cross

When a tall pea plant (TT) is crossed with a short pea plant (tt), all F₁ offspring are tall (Tt). In F₂ generation, 75% are tall and 25% are short.

5. Sex Determination

Sex determination is the process by which the sex of an individual is determined.

Definition: Sex Determination

The process by which the sex of an individual is determined by the combination of sex chromosomes.

Human Sex Determination:

  • Males: XY chromosomes
  • Females: XX chromosomes
  • Father determines the sex of the child

Sex Chromosomes:

  • X chromosome: Contains many genes
  • Y chromosome: Contains few genes
  • XX combination: Female
  • XY combination: Male

6. Evolution

Evolution is the gradual change in species over time.

Definition: Evolution

The gradual change in the characteristics of a species over several generations.

Mechanisms of Evolution:

  • Natural Selection: Survival of the fittest
  • Genetic Drift: Random changes in gene frequency
  • Mutation: Changes in DNA
  • Gene Flow: Movement of genes between populations

Example: Industrial Melanism

During the Industrial Revolution, dark-colored peppered moths became more common than light-colored ones due to natural selection.

7. Acquired and Inherited Traits

Traits can be either acquired during life or inherited from parents.

Definition: Acquired Traits

Traits that develop during an organism's lifetime and are not passed to offspring.

Definition: Inherited Traits

Traits that are passed from parents to offspring through genes.

Examples:

  • Acquired: Skills, knowledge, scars
  • Inherited: Eye color, blood group, height

8. Speciation

Speciation is the formation of new species from existing ones.

Definition: Speciation

The process by which new species are formed from existing species.

Types of Speciation:

  • Geographic Speciation: Separation by geographic barriers
  • Reproductive Speciation: Changes in reproductive behavior
  • Temporal Speciation: Separation by time

9. Evolution and Classification

Classification reflects evolutionary relationships.

Evolutionary Classification:

  • Organisms with similar characteristics are grouped together
  • Classification shows evolutionary relationships
  • More closely related organisms share more characteristics
  • Classification helps understand common ancestry

10. Human Evolution

Humans have evolved from ape-like ancestors over millions of years.

Definition: Human Evolution

The evolutionary process that led to the emergence of modern humans from ape-like ancestors.

Stages of Human Evolution:

  • Australopithecus: Early human-like species
  • Homo habilis: Tool-making humans
  • Homo erectus: Upright walking humans
  • Homo sapiens: Modern humans

Key Features of Human Evolution:

  • Bipedal locomotion (walking on two legs)
  • Increased brain size
  • Development of language
  • Use of tools and fire
