1. Magnetic Field and Field Lines

A magnetic field is a region around a magnet where magnetic forces can be detected.

Definition: Magnetic Field

A region around a magnet or current-carrying conductor where magnetic forces can be experienced.

Properties of Magnetic Field Lines:

  • They emerge from North pole and enter South pole
  • They never intersect each other
  • They are closer where field is stronger
  • They form closed loops

Diagram: Magnetic Field Lines

2. Magnetic Field due to Current-Carrying Conductor

When electric current flows through a conductor, it produces a magnetic field around it.

Definition: Oersted's Discovery

Electric current produces a magnetic field around the conductor.

Right-Hand Thumb Rule:

If you hold the conductor in your right hand with thumb pointing in the direction of current, the curled fingers show the direction of magnetic field lines.

Diagram: Right-Hand Thumb Rule

3. Magnetic Field due to Current through a Circular Loop

A current-carrying circular loop produces a magnetic field similar to a bar magnet.

Definition: Solenoid

A coil of many circular turns of insulated copper wire wrapped closely in the shape of a cylinder.

Diagram: Circular Loop and Solenoid

4. Force on a Current-Carrying Conductor in a Magnetic Field

When a current-carrying conductor is placed in a magnetic field, it experiences a force.

Definition: Fleming's Left-Hand Rule

If the forefinger, middle finger, and thumb of left hand are stretched perpendicular to each other, then forefinger shows magnetic field, middle finger shows current, and thumb shows force.

Force on Conductor:

F = BIL sin Œ∏

Where: F = force (N), B = magnetic field (T), I = current (A), L = length (m), Œ∏ = angle

5. Electric Motor

An electric motor converts electrical energy into mechanical energy using magnetic effects.

Definition: Electric Motor

A device that converts electrical energy into mechanical energy using the force on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field.

Parts of Electric Motor:

  • Armature: Coil of wire that rotates
  • Commutator: Reverses current direction
  • Brushes: Conduct electricity to armature
  • Field Magnets: Provide magnetic field

Diagram: Electric Motor

6. Electromagnetic Induction

Electromagnetic induction is the production of electric current by changing magnetic field.

Definition: Electromagnetic Induction

The production of electric current in a conductor when it is moved through a magnetic field or when magnetic field around it changes.

Induced EMF:

Œµ = -N(dŒ¶/dt)

Where: Œµ = induced EMF (V), N = number of turns, Œ¶ = magnetic flux (Wb), t = time (s)

Faraday's Laws:

  • EMF is induced when magnetic flux changes
  • Magnitude of EMF is proportional to rate of change of flux
  • Direction of EMF opposes the change causing it

7. Electric Generator

An electric generator converts mechanical energy into electrical energy.

Definition: Electric Generator

A device that converts mechanical energy into electrical energy using electromagnetic induction.

Diagram: AC Generator

8. Domestic Electric Circuits

Domestic electric circuits provide safe distribution of electricity in homes.

Definition: Domestic Circuit

The electrical wiring system that distributes electricity safely throughout a building.

Components of Domestic Circuit:

  • Main Switch: Controls entire circuit
  • MCB: Miniature Circuit Breaker
  • Fuse: Safety device that melts on overcurrent
  • Earthing: Safety connection to ground
